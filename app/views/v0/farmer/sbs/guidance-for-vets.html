Vets: how to carry out an annual livestock health and welfare review

Find out about the annual livestock health and welfare review and how vets should carry out a review.

  • Carry out testing for endemic diseases or conditions
  • Carry out a review
  • When to visit
  • Record information about the review

Defra will pay farmers to have an annual vet-led health and welfare review of their livestock.

The aim of the review is to:

  • reduce endemic diseases and conditions such as bovine viral diarrhoea (BVD) in cattle, porcine reproductive and respiratory syndrome (PRRS) in pigs and resistance to parasite treatments in sheep
  • improve the welfare of animals
  • increase productivity
  • advise farmers about veterinary medicines and vaccinations
  • help Defra understand more about the health and welfare of the national herd and flock to inform policy decisions

The review will:

  • not be an inspection or audit
  • take approximately 3 hours- this includes your time, the farmer's time, and time for testing

It's the farmer's responsibility to pay you once the review is complete.

Before you visit the farm

Before you visit the farm, the farmer will contact you to arrange a review and give you a booking reference number.

You'll need this reference number to record information after the review.

Carry out testing for endemic diseases or conditions

You must carry out testing and get any information you need from the farmer before the review. You must test for:

  • BVD in dairy and beef cattle
  • PRRS in pigs
  • the effectiveness of worming treatments in sheep

Follow guidance on testing for priority endemic diseases or conditions.

Carry out a review

You should agree with the farmer how you will carry out the review and what you will check.

Depending on the farm, you may choose to review:

  • biosecurity on the farm - check current measures and provide recommendations for improvements
  • health and welfare - work with the farmer to improve the health and productivity of their animals and give advice where there are issues such as lameness, body conditioning or mobility scoring
  • farm medicines - how to prescribe and use medicines, control and treat disease including how to register and submit data on the Agriculture and Horticulture Development Board (AHDB) Medicine Hub
    • endemic disease and condition testing - discuss test results and follow-up actions
  • grants and funding - support and advice on government grants and other funding available

At the end of the review, you must agree actions and recommendations with the farmer. This information will not be used for any regulatory or inspection purposes.

When to visit

You can spread the review over several regular visits to the farm or carry out a single visit.

A farmer may need to book multiple reviews if:

  • they have more than one animal type - for example, if they have cattle and sheep
  • have livestock in multiple places

If the farm has booked multiple reviews, you can do them in one visit or separately.

Record information about the review

You or a member of your team must record information about the review. This must be done no later than 3 months after you have visited the farm.

Use this service to tell us about a livestock review.

Recording information takes around 5 minutes.

After you submit the information, the farmer can claim funding.

You'll need to give a summary report to the farmer that includes:

  • what actions you've recommended and the farmer has agreed to
  • information about where they can find further support

You should invoice the farmer using your normal process.
