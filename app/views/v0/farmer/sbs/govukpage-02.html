Annual health and welfare reviews for cattle, sheep and pigs

Applies to England

About the review

Farmers can get funding for a vet or vet-led team to carry out an annual livestock health and welfare review.

This review is designed to:

  • reduce endemic diseases such as bovine viral disease (BVD) in cattle, porcine reproductive and respiratory disease (PRRS) in pigs and resistance to parasite treatments in sheep
  • improve the welfare of animals
  • increase productivity
  • ensure the use of veterinary medicines and vaccines is appropriate
  • be primarily for the farmer, and not used for inspections or compliance

The vet will also collect data that will be used to improve understanding of the health and welfare of the national stock. The data will not be used for inspection purposes.
