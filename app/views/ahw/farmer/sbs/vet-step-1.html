Work with the farmer to make the livestock review as useful as possible.

You can look at everything the livestock review covers or focus on specific areas that will help the most.

At the end of the livestock review, you must agree actions and recommendations with the farmer.

Health and welfare

Work with the farmer to identify health and welfare issues and provide suggestions on how to solve them.

Farm medicine use

Find out how the farmer uses medicine on livestock, including antimicrobial usage. Recommend any changes the farmer needs to make.

Discuss with the farmer how they can reduce antimicrobial use and use alternative methods to control and treat disease.

Give advice on how to submit antimicrobial use data using the Agriculture and Horticulture Development Board (AHDB) Hub.

Disease testing

Arrange for testing to be done before the review so you can discuss the results and treatment options with the farmer at the review.

Data collection

Confirm and upload animal numbers and disease test results after the review.

Offer support and advice to the farmer on government grants and other funding farmers can apply for to improve the health and welfare of their livestock.
