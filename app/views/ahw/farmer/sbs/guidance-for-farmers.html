Guidance

Farmers: get an annual livestock health and welfare review for cattle, sheep and pigs

Find out about livestock health and welfare reviews, check if you're eligible for a review, payments you'll receive and how to book a review.

You may be able to get funding for a vet or vet-led team to visit your farm and carry out a livestock health and welfare review if you keep beef or dairy cattle, sheep or pigs.

The review pays for up to 3 hours of your time and vet time to look at health and welfare on your farm.

The review will help you:

  • reduce endemic diseases and conditions such as bovine viral diarrhoea (BVD) in cattle, porcine reproductive and respiratory syndrome (PRRS) in pigs and test how effective worming treatments are in sheep
  • improve the welfare of your animals
  • increase productivity

The review will not be an inspection or be used for compliance or monitoring.

Check you're eligible for a review

You must have a minimum number of animals on the date the vet will carry out the review for each type of review you’re booking.

You must have at least:

  • 10 or more beef cattle
  • 10 or more dairy cattle
  • 20 or more sheep
  • 50 or more pigs

The livestock must be registered in England and you must be the registered keeper.

You must be eligible to claim for the Basic Payment Scheme (BPS).

You can have one review each year for each type of livestock.

How much you'll be paid

You'll be paid:

  • £522 for a beef cattle review
  • £372 for a dairy cattle review
  • £436 for a sheep review
  • £684 for a pig review

Payments are for each type of livestock, not for each animal.

Payments will help you cover the costs for:

  • the vet
  • testing for endemic diseases and conditions
  • your time

You'll be paid for each review if:

  • you have more than one type of livestock
  • the livestock are kept at different geographical locations

When you'll be paid

After the review, the vet will record information about the review. You'll receive an email to confirm you agree with the vet's record.

You'll receive payment within 28 days of confirming this.

It's your responsibility to pay the vet when the review is complete.

How to apply

You need to get a reference number so you can book a review with your vet.

Applying takes around 5 minutes.

Before the review

The vet will contact you to organise testing of your livestock before they carry out the review. The vet will test for:

  • bovine viral diarrhoea (BVD) in dairy and beef cattle
  • porcine reproductive and respiratory syndrome virus (PRRS) in pigs
  • the effectiveness of worming treatments in sheep

If you're able to do this testing, you may want to discuss this with the vet.

The vet will discuss the test results and treatment options with you when they visit.

They may ask you some questions to help them plan the review and when they'll visit.

You can agree with the vet if they'll carry out the review in a single visit or if they'll visit more than once during the review.

At the review

You will discuss the test results and any recommendations the vet makes.

You should discuss and agree with the vet what they will look at during the review.

The vet may look at:

  • biosecurity on the farm - check current measures and provide recommendations for improvements
  • health and welfare - to improve the health and productivity of your animals
  • farm medicines - how to prescribe and use medicines, control and treat disease including how to register and submit data on the Agriculture and Horticulture Development Board (AHDB) Medicine Hub
  • grants and funding - support and advice on government grants and other funding available

At the end of the review, you must agree actions and recommendations with the vet. This information will not be used for any regulatory or inspection purposes.

After the review

The vet will provide you with a summary report that includes:

  • agreed recommendations and actions to improve the health and welfare of the livestock
  • information about where to find further support

This information will not be shared with Defra or used for any regulatory or site visit purposes.

The vet, or one of their team, will record some information about the review, including test results.

Get help with your application

If you cannot apply online or have a question about your application, contact the Rural Payments Agency.

If you have your booking reference number, have it with you when you call or include it in your email.

  • Telephone: 03000 200 301
  • Monday to Friday, 8:30am to 5pm (except public holidays)
  • ruralpayments@defra.gov.uk
