Overview

You can apply for funding for a yearly vet visit that will cover:

  • a check of your biosecurity measures and advice on improving it
  • recommendations about medicines used on the farm
  • a health and welfare review
  • testing for BVD in cattle, resistance to parasite treatments in sheep and PRRSv in pigs
  • advice on access to grants and payments 

You and your vet will agree on the best actions for your farm.

The vet will also collect data that will be used to improve understanding of the health and welfare of the national stock. The data will not be used for inspection purposes.

Who is eligible

You are eligible for funding if you’re a commercial cattle, pig and sheep keeper who has:

  • received BPS (direct payments) in the last 12 months
  • at least one of the following:
    • 10 cattle
    • 20 sheep
    • 50 pigs

Payments

Payments will range from £374 to £684, depending on the type of livestock you have.

Beef cattle – £522

Dairy cattle – £374

Sheep – £436

Pigs – £684

You will receive payment in the same way you do for the Basic Payment Scheme (BPS).

What you need to do

Once you apply for funding, you will:

  • need to contact your vet to arrange a visit
  • pay the vet

Following the vet visit, it will usually take Defra about 28 days to send the funding. You may need to pay the vet before you get your funding.

How to apply

Apply for funding, it takes around 5 minutes.

Contact

If you cannot apply online or have a question about your application, contact:

Rural Payments Agency
ruralpayments@defra.gov.uk
Telephone: 03000 200 301
Monday to Friday, 8:30am to 5pm
